# 🧪 Rengeteg 2.6 – Playtest Report

## 📊 Overview

- Total responses: 2
- Focus: Short-session player impressions (first minutes of gameplay)

This report analyzes early player experience, clarity, and initial engagement with the core loop.

---

# 🎮 Player Background

## Experience with Similar Games
- Players do NOT have strong experience with:
  - Pokémon-like games
  - Roguelite / run-based games

👉 Insight:
These are **fresh players**, which means:
- feedback strongly reflects onboarding quality
- confusion is highly meaningful (not due to bad habits)

---

## Playtime
- 10–30 minutes: 1 player  
- 30–60 minutes: 1 player  

👉 Insight:
Players spent enough time to form early impressions but are still in onboarding phase.

---

# 🧭 First Impressions & Understanding

## Understanding the Game

Responses:
- “Understood some things but unsure about others”

👉 Key Issue:
The game does not clearly communicate:
- what the player should do
- how the loop works

---

## Perceived Main Goal

Player answers:
- “Improve my team”
- “Win / progress somehow”

👉 Insight:
Players sense that:
- progression exists

But they do NOT understand:
- how to achieve it
- what actions lead to success

---

# 🗺️ Exploration & Map Feel

## World Feel
- “Somewhat interesting”

## Map Structure Perception
- Neutral / unclear

👉 Key Issue:
Maps are not perceived as:
- structured
- intentional
- decision-driven

To new players, the world feels closer to:
> “a place to walk around”

rather than:
> “a space to make decisions”

---

## Player Behavior

Players reported:
- exploring
- trying to understand systems

👉 Insight:
Early gameplay is dominated by:
- learning and confusion
- not strategic decision-making

---

# ⚔️ Combat & Feedback

## Combat Understanding
- “Mostly understood”

👉 Insight:
Combat is:
- not blocking players
- but not a strong highlight either

It likely needs:
- clearer feedback
- stronger connection to outcomes

---

# 🧪 Confusion & Friction

## Reported Issues

Players mentioned:
- unclear progression
- unclear “how to win”
- lack of guidance

Direct feedback:
- “Clear understanding of how I could improve my team”
- “Simple instructions on how to win”

👉 Critical Insight:
New players need:
- **clear short-term goals**
- **explicit direction early on**

---

# ⭐ Positive Feedback

- Exploration has some appeal
- Core idea is partially understood

👉 This is good:
The concept is not rejected—it’s just unclear.

---

# ❗ Key Problems Identified

## 1. No Clear Immediate Goal
Players don’t know:
- what to do first
- what success looks like

---

## 2. Weak Onboarding
The game assumes:
- player familiarity with systems

But players actually need:
- early guidance and framing

---

## 3. Map Structure Is Invisible
Even if structure exists:
- players do not perceive it

---

## 4. Progression Is Not Explained
Players cannot connect:
- actions → outcomes

---

# ✅ What’s Working

- Players are willing to explore
- Combat is understandable
- Concept is not confusing at a high level
